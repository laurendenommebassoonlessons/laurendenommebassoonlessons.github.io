About

Originally from Metro Detroit, Lauren Denomme is a Michigan State University alumna with a Bachelor of Music degree in Performance (Bassoon). Under the instruction of Professor Michael Kroth, Lauren studied musicality, reed-making, mastery of technique, and emotive performance. Lauren enjoys the learning process and hopes to inspire middle and high school bassoon students in the South Bay area!

My Teaching Philosophy

Learning the bassoon is extremely rewarding, but it is a journey! I hope to meet a student on their journey and provide a supportive environment to foster their own performance style and instill a lifelong appreciation of music. Lessons are typically 60 minutes long and can be held in person in the South Bay area.

Foundations

Get ready to play the bassoon! We'll discuss breathing techniques, work on posture, assess your reeds (guidance with purchasing vs. making), and do some initial intonation matching and articulation exercises.

Beginner

Let's feel comfortable and confident with playing scales! I will teach students the fundamentals on learning the major and minor scales as well as learning to play the exercises from the Weissenborn bassoon method.

Band & Orchestra Repertoire

Feel free to bring your band and orchestra pieces that you are working on! I would love to help you work through any bassoon part so that you're playing in a group with ease and confidence.

Intermediate

Instruction for the aspiring college bassoonist. I will guide students through common music college audition repertoire as well as the Milde Concert etudes series.
